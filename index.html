What is BlackRoom?

A Space Where Silence Speaks

BlackRoom is a specially designed, sound-isolated room installed in educational institutions — a place where students can walk in, sit down, and share their thoughts, feelings, and struggles completely anonymously.

No cameras. No records. No judgement. Just a safe environment to let it all out.

Our Story

Where It All Began

It Didn't Start as a Product

It started as a question. During early days at a university in Udaipur, something unsettling was observed — students were present everywhere, yet emotionally absent. Classrooms were full, conversations were happening, but something deeper was missing. There was no real space where students could just be honest without fear of judgment.

They Had Things to Say

A lot to say. But no place to say it. No safe corner. No anonymous space. No system that truly listened without labeling. That's when an idea emerged — What if we created a space where identity didn't matter, but expression did? A space that feels different the moment you enter. Quiet. Isolated. Safe. Almost like stepping outside the noise of the world.

That Idea Became BlackRoom

They tested it first in that very environment — inside the university. A simple setup. Minimal structure. But a powerful intention. Two rooms covered with black curtains, a call integration using intercom, a psychologist in one room, and an open room for students to visit. It was raw, minimal, and deeply intentional.

Something Unexpected Happened

Students started opening up. Not just surface-level thoughts, but real emotions — stress, confusion, pressure, things they had never said out loud before. It wasn't just usage. It was release. BlackRoom wasn't just a concept — it was something students genuinely needed.
